## What to review

The good version uses a native `button`, which already participates in keyboard interaction, focus order, and accessibility APIs.

The bad version may be styled to look identical, but the generic `div` has to recreate expected button behavior by hand.

## Review notes

In review, ask whether the element's semantics match the user's action. A clickable `div` is review-worthy even when the click handler works because it quietly shifts keyboard, focus, disabled, and pressed-state responsibilities into custom code.
