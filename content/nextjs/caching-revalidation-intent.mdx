## What to review

The good version states that published posts can be cached for hours and tagged for targeted revalidation.

The bad version relies on an unstated assumption that framework fetches are cached, which is not true by default in this Next.js version.

## Review notes

In review, ask whether the data should be fresh per request, cached for a period, or revalidated by mutation. The bad version is review-worthy because hidden caching assumptions cause either slow pages or stale content depending on how the app evolves.
